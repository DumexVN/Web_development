My achievements
1. Translanguaging Competition At the start of 2016, I was announced qualifying for the semi-final of the school "Translanguaging Competition". I made it through to the final but I was shocked. My Head of English informed that the final was changed from a worksheet to a live public speaking contest. Public speaking had been one of my greatest fear at school since I was always shy, talking in front of big groups of people. However, there was no stepping back from this. I practiced speaking to a mirror and then my family. I forced myself not to stutter when speaking and spent half an hour each day, researching new vocabulary. During the finale, my confidence was boosted knowing my topic was science. At the end, my hardwork paid off.
2. Maths Challenge As a maths-lover, I am prepared to partcipate in any maths competition. Not only pushing my mind to the limits, Maths competition is also a great way to learn from other mathematicians. Entering the contest, the High School team was one person short. Consequently, my teacher decided to bring me up from the Senior Team. Considering underdogs because we were the youngest, our oldest member was in year 10 whereas the contest was for students from year 10 to 13, we proved them wrong. We tried our best, concentrated as hard as we could and our phenomenal efforts resulted in the third place, only one point short of the Silver medals.
3. Music As I have mentioned above, my glossophobia has got better from the Translanguaging Competition. However, I want to push myself even harder. I have signed up to the World Scholar's Cup, which is an international competition of debating. This requires fluent English, a great reaction time and a huge amount of knowledge. The qualifying rounds will be held locally, whereas the next round, which is international round will be held in Indonesia. I have been practicing with my team to improve our research, summary and persuasive skills. We will try our best to make it to the finale, which will be held at Yale University.

My achievements
1. Project Euler Projecteuler.net is a website designed specifically to enhace the users' skills in Mathematics and Coding. I have been working on this website for approximately twelve months. At first, I could not see the relationship between these two subjects. "Why should we write codes to solve Mathematic problems when we can create games to play?". Later on, I have realised that if I have not had a decent knowledge of Maths, I would not have completed this many questions. Furthermore, coding has trained my mentality to be better at concentrating and my attention to details have also improved massively.
2. My Profile Website Designing this website has taken me a lot of time and effort. Not only that I have had to planned the contents of my website, but also, I have had to designed the interface as well as interactive features with readers that I should have added. This has not been an easy task to me. I have had to research the languages which should be used in this, including Javascript, PHP, and the fundamental syntax of a website. I have obtained a greater knowledge of Javascript as a result, as well as being concise in any situation.
3. Music Playing music have been my favourite hobby during my freetime. I have been learning piano since I was 9 years old. Some might think piano is boring since all you need to do is press a key and a sound is made. To me, playing the piano is more about paying attention to the smallest sounds and passion. To play it well, you do not just spend hours and hours of practicing but you also have to put your heart and souls in the compositions. Drums is another instrument I enjoy playing. Playing drums is more about discovering new techniques than practice and practice. The drummer is the most important person in a band, in terms of keeping every musicians in the same rhythm. This requires a high concentration level and great confidence.
4. Duke of Edinburgh Overcoming greatest fears is my number one aim in life. For anyone to survive in the jungle after being stranded is almost impossible. That is why I choose to participate in this programme. Designed to push students to their limits, this programme has changed my view about survival adventures. I have learnt build my own raft, making my own tent, improve my fitness but more importantly, I have figured out how to motivate other when they try to give up. Until now, I have completed the Bronze certificate and in progress for the Silver Award.
5. Operation Smile I am very fortunate to be learning in the perfect environment. Seeing 12-months-old kids suffering from disabilities makes me question what can I do for them. Last summer, I have found myself a place in Operation Smile Vietnam and for the first time, I compromised with the manager for a trip to Quang Ngai. My team, went to local schools for six days and brought them a better future. We screened for more than a thousand kids and performed more than 300 extractions whereas the surgical team performed more than 50 cleft palates operations. It was the most meaningful trip I have ever been on. I am looking forward to the next summer mission in 2017.
6. Sports Sports is not only a good way to relax but it forms a great bond between players. To be good at sports, you cannot practice by yourself, you need your peers to support you and giving you feedback. The most important factor in team sports is teamwork, even elite players from top teams all over the globe are defeated when they do not play as a team. However, to be a good team, you need a conductor, someone who steps up to be in charge, control what is happening on the court and motivates others when needed. Playing sports have given all of the skills required to be a good teamplayer. I play any sports that is available at my school. However, I enjoy football, basketball and handball the most since they are team sports which require high cognitive skills.
